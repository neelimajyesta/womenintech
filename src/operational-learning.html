AI Stitch — live walkthrough 🧵

Watch how AI stitches into the injury-reporting workflow, end to end.

STEP 01Worker reports
STEP 02AI scans corpus
STEP 03Pattern surfaced
STEP 04Summary drafted
STEP 05Actions suggested
Step 01 — worker

Tap the body map. Structured data, zero friction.

A line operator pulls up the EHSS app on a shop-floor tablet. They tap exactly where it hurts. The app captures structured incident data — body part, shift, station, severity — without forcing them to write a paragraph.

CAPTURED
Left wrist · Station B-12 · 06:42 AM
operator_tenure: 47 days · shift: early · pain_level: 6/10 · task: rivet-gun cycle

No prose required. The worker is back to work in under 90 seconds.

Step 02 — AI

Claude scans 12 months of incident reports.

The new report drops into a queue. A background agent vectorizes the body-part, shift, station, and operator-tenure features, then runs similarity search across 4,217 historical incidents. Anomalies and clusters get flagged.

Step 03 — pattern

Three wrist injuries. Same station. 30 days.

Hand-curated rules would have missed this. The model surfaces a cluster that no single supervisor was in a position to see.

CLUSTER
3× left-wrist · Station B-12
window: 30 days · same shift · operator tenure < 90d
CONFIDENCE
93.4%
grounded in 11 prior incident reports · 2 toolbox-talk transcripts
LIKELY CAUSE
Rivet-gun cycle time
new SOP shortened cycle by 1.4s on 2026-02-14 — wrist incidents rose afterward
SUGGESTED
Convene Learning Team
non-punitive review · pull operators, supervisor, ergonomist
Step 04 — synthesis

Learning Team summary, drafted in seconds.

The agent transcribes the Learning Team call, grounds the draft against the original incident reports, then writes the systemic summary that humans review and sign off on.

Step 05 — close the loop

From summary to system change.

Action items get drafted, assigned, and tracked. The loop closes — and the trust loop opens.
